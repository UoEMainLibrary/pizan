Prologue adreçant à la royne

Folios 3r-3v of the Queen's MS contain the only known copy of the Prologue adreçant à la royne in which Christine de Pizan dedicates her collection to Queen Isabeau. The poem's importance is highlighted by the frontispiece on f. 3r: the miniature spanning columns a and b is framed by a richly elaborated border. The scene is the Queen's bedroom where Christine kneels to present her book to Isabeau who is accompanied by six of her ladies.

The poem has no rubric; the opening line being written immediately under the miniature. The title just quoted is taken from the Table (f. 2c). The Prologue is made up of eight douzains in octosyllables rhyming aabaabbaabaa ccdccdddcddc etc.; none of the sixteen rhymes is repeated.

The opening stanza is introduced by a decorated capital 'T' for which a five-line space has been reserved. The first line of all but one of the other stanzas is enhanced, now by an elaborate initial penned by the scribe (stanzas 2, 5, 7-8), now by a paragraph mark (stanzas 4-8), now in both ways (stanzas 5, 7-8). The beginning of stanza 3 has no distinguishing feature.

Blank lines have been inserted between the stanzas in this edition; there are none in the manuscript.
