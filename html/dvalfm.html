Le Livre du duc des vrais amans

The Livre du duc des vrais amans is divided into 6 sections:

1) the Livre (DVAL), a narrative in heptasyllabic couplets, interspersed with formes fixes (15 ballades, 3 rondeaux, 1 virelai) and 8 letters in prose.

2) an epilogue (DVAE) made up of 12 heptasyllabic couplets.

3) a collection of 9 Balades de plusieurs façons (DVAB).

4) a collection of 3 Virelais (DVAV).

5) a collection of 4 Rondiaulx (DVAR).

6) a Complainte (DVAC).

The Livre du duc des vrais amans is extant in only two manuscripts, London, BL, Harley 4431 andParis, BnF, f.fr. 836. The work was edited by Maurice Roy in 1896 (Oeuvres poétiques de Christine de Pisan, Paris: SATF, vol. 3, pp. 59-208), who based his text on the Paris MS. A new edition, based on the Harley MS, was published by Thelma S. Fenster in 1995: (Christine de Pizan: Le Livre du duc des vrais amans, Binghamton, NY: Medieval & Renaissance texts & studies 124, 261 pp.). The verse lines are numbered in both editions. From line 2066 onwards Fenster's line numbers, which are used here, are greater by one than those in Roy's edition, because Fenster numbers the missing line, discussed below.

The copy of the Livre du duc des vrais amans in the Queen's MS is carefully executed; the text is notably free of abbreviations. The errors and emendations made by the scribe and the reviser's corrections are surprisingly few in number. Lines of verse have been copied twice on ff. 151c and 174b, the second copy being scored out. The scribe has left a blank line on fol. 157c to show that line 2067 is missing.

The work contains five 'split rhymes': The first syllable(s) of the rhyme-word provide the rhyme and the remaining syllable(s) are carried over to the next line. Two of the split rhymes are adverbs ending in –ment (lines 1513-4, 1598-9), two are a form of avoir (lines 2055-6, 2385-6); the last example is tour–noyé (lines 953-4) . The scribe has copied -Ment once with a capital; in all the other instances the new line begins with a small letter, which has been retained.

The refrains of the ballades, are written now in full, now in abbreviated form. The refrains of virelais rondeaux are presented differently: they are always abbreviated and always inset. Both layouts have been reproduced, as has the use - and sometimes the absence - of blank lines. The majority of the errors in the text have been corrected by reference to BnF, f.fr. 836.

The refrains have been set in italic, in accordance with modern practice.
